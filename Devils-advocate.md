The Devil’s Advocate: Why Some People Fight AI So Aggressively

Not everyone who fears AI is afraid of technology.

Some are afraid of losing the systems that protect their advantage.

For years, many industries have operated on:
```code
subjective judgment

personal networks

gatekeeping

bias

stereotypes

selective rules

inconsistent standards

These systems allowed certain people to:

hide their weaknesses

inflate their strengths

control opportunities

maintain power through ambiguity

benefit from “who you know” instead of “what you can do”
```
AI threatens that world — not because it is dangerous, but because it introduces something many people quietly fear:
```code
Objectivity.
Consistency.
Transparency.
Skill amplification.
Equal access to knowledge.
```
When a person uses AI to:
```code
write more clearly

think more structurally

explore more ideas

improve their communication

upskill faster

compete fairly

overcome language barriers

present themselves professionally
```
…it removes the advantage of those who relied on subjective systems to stay ahead.

This is why some people paint AI as “dirty,” “dangerous,” or “evil.”
Not because the technology is harmful — but because fairness feels threatening to those who benefited from unfairness.

AI does not destroy human value.
It destroys:
```code
bias‑based gatekeeping

stereotype‑driven decisions

inconsistent rules

hidden power structures

the idea that only a few deserve access to clarity

And that is uncomfortable for people who relied on those systems.

```
A world with more clarity, more objectivity, and more equal access is not a threat to humanity — it is a threat only to inequality.
